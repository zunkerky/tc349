Kyle Zunker Professional Biography

Kyle Zunker is currently a student at Michigan State University.

He is pursuing an undergraduate degree in Media and Information, with a specilization in Information Technology, at the College of Communication Arts and Sciences. Kyle has long had a passion for computers and related electronic equipment. The capabilities of these devices have always fascinated him. Kyle would like to pursue a career in the field of Information Technology. What started out as a curiosity and a hobby has now turned into a career choice. The combination of both being passionate about computer technology, and what seems like a stable industry, are what has drawn Kyle to this industry.

The specific areas of IT that most interest Kyle are the following:

  • Computer Networking
  • IT Security
  • Systems Analysis/Design
  • Databases

While Kyle has an interest in just about any topic surrounding IT, these mentioned areas interest Kyle the most because, the more he learns about those topics the more interested he becomes. The topics above play an important role in any most every IT setting and Kyle would like to contribute in these areas at a company, government, or other organization.

Kyle continues to gain experience in many areas of IT while working at DeWitt Computer Center. At DCC Kyle is part of a team that supports both small businesses and residential computer users in the Lansing area. He has been included in projects that have dealt with removing computer viruses, setting up small computer networks, configuring various pieces of software, and many more tasks surrounding IT support.

Aside from Kyle's academic and professional experiences he has many hobbies.

Some of these hobbies include:

  • Building computers and tinkering with electronics
  • Watching MSU sporting events and Green Bay Packers football
  • Spending time with his friends outside hiking and camping
  • Reading great books and watching great movie/TV shows

Panic.com Critique

Panic.com's webpage immediatley stood out to me because of the simple and user friendly UI on their homepage. Panic explains in large text exactly who they are and what they do on their homepage. The color scheme is easy on the eyes, using a fairly light background and dark text. In their opening statement they highlight their name in red making the name Panic stand out and stick in your mind.

With large icons that pop up and explain each of the pieces of software the company offers, even a user who was not familiar with Panic or their software would have a basic understanding of who they are and their offerings just by looking at the home page.

All of Panic's webpages are very nice and clean looking although I do see some issues. The main issue for me is that the software Panic provides is for a more advanced computer user compaired to someone who just surfs the web, checks their email and facebook, etc. With these more complicated and advanced pieces of software the provided documentation is fairly basic (although that can be a positive as well for new users). The other issue I found with the website was that it was somewhat difficult to navigate the Blog portion of the site. On the blog there is no clear method of searching for content and articles related to a specific piece of software they offer.

Other than the two minor issues I saw with Panic's site, the site is very visually appealing and easy to navigate. They have easy access on their pages to support via email and they also list useful additional contact info including their address and phone number.
